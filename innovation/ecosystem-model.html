The Framework

A complete map of every dimension that drives innovation success.

Most innovation initiatives fail not because the ideas are bad — but because critical dimensions of the ecosystem go unmapped and underinvested. The Ecosystem Model: 7 Core Elements™ was developed by ENTEVATE to ensure nothing falls through the cracks.

Rather than treating innovation as a single-track challenge, the 7 Core Elements™ maps the full environment: policy, research, infrastructure, economic growth, workforce, venture formation, and community access. Each element is assessed, gap-analyzed, and integrated into a shared, milestone-driven roadmap.

The 7 Core Elements Basecamps™

Every dimension. One framework.

The 7 Core Elements™ maps the full innovation ecosystem across seven interconnected basecamps — no dimension is treated in isolation, and no stakeholder is left unaccounted for.

Venture Development
The beating heart of any innovation ecosystem — supporting the formation, growth, and scaling of ventures born from research, collaboration, and institutional investment. This is where ideas become organizations.
Policy / Advocacy
Shaping the regulatory frameworks, government partnerships, and institutional policies that enable innovation to take root, scale, and benefit the broader community it serves.
Research & Innovation Development
Building the research infrastructure, academic partnerships, and applied R&D pipelines that generate breakthroughs and move them toward real-world commercialization and impact.
Real Estate / Infrastructure / Placemaking
Creating the physical and digital environments — labs, campuses, innovation districts — where frontier technology work can happen at the scale and quality the work demands.
Economic Development
Connecting innovation activity to regional economic growth through job creation, industry attraction, startup formation, and structured commercialization pathways that benefit the broader economy.
Workforce Development
Building the talent pipeline through education, training, reskilling, and credentialing programs that prepare communities for a technology-enabled economy — and ensure no one is left behind.
Social / Community / Accessibility
Ensuring innovation benefits are broadly shared through intentional community engagement, equity frameworks, and accessible pathways into the innovation economy for all residents and stakeholders.
